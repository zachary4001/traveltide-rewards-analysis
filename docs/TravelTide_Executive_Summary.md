The analysis covers the full active customer cohort of **5,998 users** who completed seven or more platform sessions since January 2023.

### Perk Assignment Results

Every user was assigned exactly one perk based on demonstrated behavioral signals, not demographic assumptions. Assignment uses a lifecycle-gated model: users with fewer than three completed trips receive simplified assignments appropriate to their stage, while users with three or more trips are evaluated across all five behavioral dimensions. Perk assignments are designed to be **dynamic**, they update as user behavior evolves on the platform.

</div></div></div></div></div></div>### Business Implications

**Revenue concentration:** The top 20% of spenders generate 44% of total cohort revenue ($7,613 average vs $833 for the bottom 20%). Retaining the Premium Travel Benefits group protects the majority of platform revenue.

**Lifecycle progression opportunity:** If 10% of users in each tier progressed one tier up, the projected revenue uplift is **+$1,049,937**. Two-tier progression yields a combined **+$1,780,927**, from customers already on the platform.

**Discount dependency is a minority behavior:** 73.7% of all bookings use no discount. Offering discounts to non-discount-dependent users erodes margin without driving behavior change. The perk model targets discounts only where the data supports it.

**International travel premium:** International flights represent 38.4% of flight bookings but carry a 49% fare premium per seat ($453 vs $350). International travelers also book 22 days in advance vs 11 days for domestic, a high-value, planful segment.

**Peak booking window:** 7PM–8PM is the single highest booking hour (1,584 bookings). Activity builds steadily from 4PM and peaks between 7PM–9PM daily. Promotional email send timing should align to this window.

**Cancellation risk is concentrated:** Total cancelled gross spend across the cohort is $1,364,992. The 321 high-cancel users and 88 total-cancellers represent the concentrated risk core, directly targeted by the No Change Fees perk.

### Recommended Next Steps

**01 - A/B Test: 30 Day Pilot** Randomly sample 50% of cohort users per perk group. Test group receives one personalized perk email per week for 30 days. Control group receives standard generic promotional email. Average booking lead time is under two weeks, results are measurable within the test window.

**02 - Formalize the Loyalty Tier Program** Make the lifecycle tiers visible to customers as a named loyalty program based on completed (non-cancelled) trips. Tier progression creates a self-reinforcing conversion incentive and a clear narrative for future marketing campaigns.

**03 - Review Cancellation UX** 75.9% of cancellation sessions exceed one hour in duration, 56.6% hit the 2-hour system timeout exactly. The cancellation flow does not actively close sessions on completion. An IT review here reduces data noise and likely improves the customer experience.

**04 - Quarterly Perk Threshold Review** Behavioral thresholds are data-driven and should be reviewed quarterly as user behavior evolves. Key trigger: cohort expansion to EMEA or significant shift in cancellation baseline. The 80% international threshold (34 additional users) is the first adjustment candidate for Free Checked Bags.
